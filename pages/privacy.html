Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Global Macro Dash ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use our website at https://global-macro-dash.vercel.app (the "Service"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and operating with full transparency. Please read this document carefully so you understand exactly how we handle data in connection with your use of the Service.

1. Information We Collect

Global Macro Dash is built as a privacy-respecting, predominantly client-side application. We minimize data collection to what is genuinely necessary to operate and improve the Service:

  • Browser Local Storage: Your language preference and a session visitor counter are stored in your browser's localStorage. This data exists only on your own device and is never transmitted to our servers. You can clear it at any time through your browser's storage settings.
  • Anonymous Usage Analytics: We collect aggregated, anonymous usage data to understand which features users find most useful and to identify technical issues. This includes page view counts, feature interaction events (such as currency converter usage, table sorting, language changes), and error logs. No personally identifiable information is included. Analytics data may be sent to a logging service such as a Google Sheets API webhook, and is retained for up to 12 months before deletion.
  • Advertising and Tracking Data: Our third-party advertising partners (Google AdSense and Adsterra) collect data about your browsing behavior to serve personalized advertisements. This data collection is described in detail in Section 3 below and is governed by those partners' own privacy policies.
  • Voluntary Contact Information: If you email us at taeshinkim11@gmail.com, we receive your email address and the content of your message. We use this only to respond to your inquiry. We do not add you to any marketing list, share your email with third parties, or retain email correspondence beyond the period necessary to address your inquiry.

2. How We Use Information

We use the limited information we collect for the following purposes only:

  • To remember your language preference and provide a consistent, personalized interface experience across visits without requiring account registration.
  • To fetch exchange rate, commodity, and macro-economic data from third-party APIs on your behalf and display it within the dashboard.
  • To understand usage patterns, identify the most valuable features, and make informed decisions about future improvements to the Service.
  • To display advertising that supports the free operation of the Service and allows us to continue providing it at no charge.
  • To respond to support requests, bug reports, and feedback submitted via email.

We do not sell, rent, or trade any user data to third parties for their own marketing purposes. We do not use your data to build personal profiles or engage in behavioral targeting beyond what our advertising partners do as described below.

3. Cookies and Advertising

Global Macro Dash does not set first-party cookies. We use browser localStorage for preference storage, which is not accessible by third-party scripts. However, our advertising partners use cookies and related tracking technologies:

  • Google AdSense: We display advertisements served by Google AdSense (publisher ID: ca-pub-7098271335538021). Google uses the DoubleClick cookie and other tracking technologies to serve ads based on your visits to this site and other sites across the internet. This is known as interest-based or personalized advertising. Google's use of advertising cookies allows it and its partners to show you ads that may be more relevant to your interests. You may opt out of Google's personalized advertising at any time by visiting Google Ads Settings or by using the NAI opt-out tool at optout.networkadvertising.org. For more information about Google's data practices in connection with ads, please see How Google uses data when you use our partners' sites or apps.
  • Adsterra: We also serve advertisements through Adsterra, including banner ads, native advertising units, and popunder formats. Adsterra uses cookies and tracking pixels to serve, optimize, and report on advertising performance. For information about Adsterra's data collection and your choices, please review Adsterra's Privacy Policy.

You can manage or disable cookies at any time through your browser settings. Most browsers offer options to block all cookies, accept only first-party cookies, or be notified before cookies are set. Please note that disabling advertising cookies will not remove advertising from the Service but may result in less relevant ads being shown. Additionally, your device's operating system and browser may offer additional privacy controls, such as iOS App Tracking Transparency, that affect how advertising networks collect data on mobile devices.

4. Third-Party Services

The Service integrates with the following third parties, each with their own privacy practices:

  • Frankfurter API / European Central Bank: Exchange rate data is fetched from this API. Only your requested currency parameters are sent. No personal data is transmitted. See Frankfurter.app.
  • Google AdSense: Advertising network. See Google Privacy Policy.
  • Adsterra: Advertising network. See Adsterra Privacy Policy.
  • Vercel: Hosting provider. See Vercel Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts: Font CDN. IP addresses may be logged. See Google Privacy Policy.
  • Chart.js (jsDelivr CDN): Charting library loaded from jsDelivr CDN. See jsDelivr Privacy Policy.

5. Data Retention

Data stored in your browser's local storage persists until you clear it via your browser settings — there is no automatic expiration. Anonymous analytics data sent to our logging endpoint is retained for a maximum of 12 months, after which it is deleted. Email correspondence is retained only as long as needed to address your inquiry and is then deleted. We do not maintain a database of user-submitted personal information.

6. Your Rights Under GDPR (European Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and equivalent legislation grant you specific rights with respect to your personal data. Because we collect minimal personal data, many of these rights are exercised primarily through your browser settings or our advertising partners' opt-out tools. Your GDPR rights include:

  • Right of Access (Article 15): You have the right to request information about what personal data, if any, we hold about you and how it is used.
  • Right to Erasure (Article 17): You can delete all locally stored data by clearing your browser's local storage. For any server-side data, contact us and we will delete it within 30 days.
  • Right to Object (Article 21): You may object to the processing of your data for advertising purposes using the opt-out mechanisms provided by Google and Adsterra.
  • Right to Data Portability (Article 20): You may request a copy of any personal data we hold about you in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18): In certain circumstances, you may request that we restrict the processing of your personal data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at taeshinkim11@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Your Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) provides you with specific rights. We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA. You have the right to: know what personal information is collected, used, or disclosed; request deletion of personal information; opt out of the sale of personal information (not applicable here); and be free from discrimination for exercising your CCPA rights. To submit a CCPA request, contact us at taeshinkim11@gmail.com.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for or directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. The macro-economic and financial data presented on this Service is intended for adult users with financial literacy. If you believe a child under 13 has provided personal information through the Service, please contact us immediately and we will take appropriate action to delete the information.

9. Security

We implement reasonable security measures to protect the Service and any data associated with it. The Service is delivered over HTTPS, ensuring data in transit is encrypted. However, no method of internet transmission is 100% secure. Because most user-specific data is stored in your own browser's local storage, your primary data security tool is your own device security — keeping your device and browser up to date and using secure, trusted networks when accessing financial information.

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal obligations, or for other operational reasons. When changes are made, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page whenever you visit the Service. Continued use of the Service following the posting of changes constitutes your acceptance of those changes.
