About Global Macro Dash

Global Macro Dash is a free, browser-based macro-economic data visualization dashboard that brings together exchange rates, commodity prices, currency conversion, and key economic indicators from around the world into a single, intuitive interface. Designed for financial analysts, economists, investors, business professionals, and students of international economics, the platform makes complex global financial data accessible and easy to understand — without subscriptions, logins, or fees.

Why Macro-Economic Data Matters

Macro-economic conditions shape virtually every significant financial decision made by individuals, corporations, and governments. Exchange rates determine the profitability of international trade and the real value of cross-border investments. Inflation rates signal purchasing power trends and influence central bank interest rate policy. GDP growth trajectories reveal which economies are expanding and which are contracting. Unemployment figures reflect labor market health, consumer confidence, and future spending potential. For anyone involved in international business, investing, portfolio management, or economic research, monitoring these indicators consistently is not optional — it is essential.

Consider a few concrete use cases: an import-export business needs to track EUR/USD and USD/JPY movements daily to time foreign currency purchases. A fixed-income investor monitors central bank interest rate decisions across twelve major economies to assess bond market risk. A multinational corporation's treasury department tracks inflation in each country it operates in to adjust local pricing strategies. A graduate student studying comparative economics needs quick access to GDP growth, unemployment, and inflation data for multiple countries simultaneously. Global Macro Dash is built precisely for these use cases — providing a clean, fast, reliable overview without the complexity of professional Bloomberg terminals or the paywalls of financial data platforms.

Data Sources and Reliability

We are committed to transparency about where our data comes from:

  • Exchange Rates: All foreign exchange rates are sourced from the European Central Bank (ECB) via the Frankfurter API. The ECB publishes official reference rates for major currency pairs on each business day (Monday through Friday) at approximately 16:00 Central European Time. These rates are widely used as benchmarks in international finance, contracts, and regulatory reporting. Because they are published once per business day, rates do not change in real time throughout the trading session.
  • Commodity Prices: Gold, silver, and crude oil price data is derived from market sources and expressed in relation to currency rates available through the ECB. Commodity prices are volatile and can change rapidly throughout the trading day. Prices shown may not reflect the most current tick-level market price and should be treated as indicative.
  • Macro-Economic Indicators: GDP growth rates, consumer price inflation, central bank interest rates, and unemployment rates for major economies are compiled from publicly available data published by the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the World Bank, the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), and national statistics agencies. These figures represent the most recently published official data at the time of our last database update and may not reflect the most current quarterly or monthly releases.

Key Features

  • Live Exchange Rate Cards: Nine major currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CHF, USD/CAD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD, USD/CNY, USD/KRW) displayed as individual cards with current rate, absolute change, percentage change, and color-coded directional indicator.
  • 7-Day Sparkline Trend Charts: Each currency card includes a miniature sparkline chart showing the 7-day price history, giving immediate visual context for whether a currency pair is trending up, down, or sideways over the past week.
  • Interactive Currency Converter: A full-featured currency converter supporting 30+ currencies. Enter any amount, select source and target currencies from dropdown menus, and see the converted result instantly. Quick shortcut buttons are provided for the most common conversion pairs. Swap currencies with a single click.
  • Commodity Price Tracker: Gold (XAU/USD), silver (XAG/USD), and crude oil (WTI) price cards with trend indicators, providing a quick macro market overview alongside foreign exchange data.
  • Macro Indicators Table: A sortable table covering twelve major economies including the United States, Eurozone, United Kingdom, Japan, China, India, Canada, Australia, South Korea, Brazil, Switzerland, and others, displaying GDP growth rate, inflation rate, central bank interest rate, unemployment rate, and local currency.
  • Auto-Refresh: The dashboard automatically refreshes exchange rate data every five minutes, with a countdown timer displayed in the header so you always know when the next update is coming. Manual refresh is also available.
  • 8-Language Interface: The dashboard is fully localized in English, Korean, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified), Spanish, German, French, and Portuguese.
  • Social Sharing: Share current rate summaries directly to X/Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn, or copy a shareable link to the clipboard.

  • Corporate Treasury and Finance Teams: Professionals managing foreign exchange exposure, hedging programs, or international cash management use the dashboard to monitor rate movements and macro conditions affecting their currency holdings.
  • Economists and Financial Analysts: Researchers and analysts who regularly compare macro indicators across multiple countries appreciate having GDP, inflation, interest rate, and unemployment data for twelve major economies in a single sortable table.
